Third Simulation: Trailer

Posted on Thu 09 June 2022 in Article

Quick Trailer Simulation

A towed trailer can have the potential to be unstable under certain loading conditions. A stability criteria can be derived from the simple model shown below

Sometimes called a 2-dof trailer model, this particular model allows the vehicle to be represented with two coordinates. With bond graphs, we generate four equations of motion for simulation: three energy storing states and one state that is necessary for describing the angle of the trailer.

The model can be represented with a bond graph as shown below.

The stability criteria (not derived here) is \(mab > J_y\). We should be able to show this in the simulation

Stable Vehicle Results

For the first simulation \(mab = 1875\) and \(J_y = 937\) at a forward speed of 30 mph. The plots below show that with an initial trailer angle of 1 degree, the trailer damps down. The spring stiffness was chosen such that the displacement is not too huge.

Unstable Vehicle Results

For the second simulation the moment of inertia is increased to \(J_y = 2343\). What is apparent is that the trailer angle grows.

This does not mean that this is exactly what would happen in a real vehicle because of the assumptions made in the model. Small angle approximations, linear tires, etc.

However, it is good indicator as to why one should follow the directions when loading a trailer. Load the trailer such that it is "tongue heavy" and try not to transport items sticking out the back as this increases the moment of inertia.
